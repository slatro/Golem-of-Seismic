# Seismic Golem - One-Page GDD

## High Concept
Seismic Golem is a stylized 3D roguelike dungeon crawler where the player controls a stone golem descending into living underground ruins. The character is always the exact stone golem silhouette provided in the concept art, with the exact pink faceted 3D logo embedded in its chest as the Core. Each run lets the player evolve through stone-body upgrades and Core mutations, creating different elemental builds while preserving the same iconic character identity.

## Pillars
1. Heavy, satisfying melee combat with seismic impact.
2. A single iconic hero whose body mutates without losing silhouette.
3. Clear run-based build variety through Body and Core evolution.
4. Stylized, readable underground biomes with strong replayability.

## Player Fantasy
The player should feel like an ancient stone guardian powered by a dangerous crystalline Core. Every upgrade should make the golem feel more unstable, more powerful, and more geologically alive.

## Character Rules
- The playable hero is always the exact golem from the provided concept.
- The chest Core is always the exact pink faceted logo from the provided image.
- Build changes affect materials, cracks, glow, particles, and attachments.
- The main body proportions and silhouette do not fundamentally change.

## Core Gameplay Loop
1. Enter a run.
2. Clear combat rooms in an underground floor.
3. Choose upgrades from Body mutations, Core mutations, or relics.
4. Fight elites and biome guardians.
5. Descend deeper into more dangerous layers.
6. Die and return with permanent progression resources.

## Combat Loop
- Light combo: reliable close-range damage.
- Heavy strike: slow, armor-breaking impact.
- Dash: short reposition with collision weight.
- Core skill: chest-powered active ability.
- Ultimate: temporary Core overload state.

Combat goals:
- Strong hit-stop and screen shake.
- Dust, rock debris, and crystal energy feedback.
- Slow but powerful attack pacing.
- Easy-to-read enemy telegraphs and room flow.

## Progression Structure
### In-Run Progression
- Body Mutations: modify melee behavior, defense, and material state.
- Core Mutations: modify active skills, procs, and elemental expressions.
- Relics: alter rules, synergies, and playstyle.

### Meta Progression
- Unlock new mutations and relic pools.
- Increase starting survivability and Core efficiency.
- Unlock later biomes and event rooms.

## Starting Build Paths
### Magma Golem
- High burst melee.
- Burn and lava shockwaves.
- Strong area control at close range.

### Frost Crystal Golem
- Slow, freeze, and shard control.
- Safer, more defensive combat rhythm.

### Toxic Ore Golem
- Damage-over-time, puddles, sustain.
- Strong attrition-based play.

### Pure Energy Golem
- Chain reactions, high tempo, volatile power.
- Lower durability but explosive skill synergy.

## World Structure

- Biome 2: Crystal Vein Hollows
- Biome 3: Molten Fault Deep

Room types:
- Combat
- Elite
- Core Shrine
- Stone Forge
- Risk Room
- Merchant
- Boss Gate

## Enemy Direction
Enemy roles should pressure movement, timing, and build choices.

Initial enemy set:
- Shard Mite: fast swarm unit
- Tunnel Brute: heavy melee bruiser
- Vein Caster: ranged crystal attacker
- Toxic Burrower: ambush and area denial
- Obsidian Sentinel: elite shielded threat

## Boss Direction
- Biome 1 boss: Quarry Keeper
- Biome 2 boss: Vein Mother
- Biome 3 boss: The Deep Fault

Bosses should feel like geological disasters rather than standard monsters.

## Art Direction
- Stylized 3D with readable chunky forms.
- Underground spaces built from stone, crystal, ore, lava, and ruins.
- Darker environments that make the pink Core visually iconic.
- Character readability is more important than hyper-detail.

## Audio Direction
- Stone scraping, heavy impacts, deep rumbles.
- Crystal resonance and faceted energy tones from the Core.
- Element-specific sound layers for magma, frost, toxic, and energy builds.

## MVP Scope
- 1 playable character
- 1 biome
- 4 enemy types
- 1 miniboss
- 1 biome boss
- 2 build paths
- 1 complete run flow
- Basic meta progression

## Success Criteria
- The golem feels powerful within the first minute.
- The Core is visually and mechanically memorable.
- Two runs can feel meaningfully different through build choices.
- A 15-20 minute run is fun from start to finish.
